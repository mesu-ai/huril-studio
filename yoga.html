Yoga

    What Is Yoga?

    Yoga is an ancient and complex practice, rooted in Indian philosophy. It started as a spiritual practice but has grown in popularity as a way to promote physical and mental health. Although classical yoga also includes other elements, yoga practiced in the United States often emphasizes physical postures (asanas), breathing techniques (pranayama), and meditation (dyana). There are many different styles of yoga, from gentle exercises to physically demanding ones. Differences in the types of yoga used in the studies may affect the study results. Therefore, it is difficult to evaluate research on the health effects of yoga. Yoga and two practices that originated in China, tai chi and qigong, are sometimes referred to as “meditative movements” practices. All three practices include both meditative and physical elements.

What are the health benefits of yoga?

Research suggests that yoga may:

  1. Help improve general wellness by relieving stress, supporting good health habits, and improving mental/emotional health, sleep, and balance.
  2. Help people who are overweight or obese lose weight.
  3. Help people manage anxiety or depressive symptoms associated with difficult life situations.
  4. Help people with chronic diseases manage their symptoms and improve their quality of life.
  5. Relieve low-back pain and neck pain, and possibly pain from tension-type headaches and knee osteoarthritis.
  6. Help people quit smoking.
  7. Relieve menopause symptoms.
