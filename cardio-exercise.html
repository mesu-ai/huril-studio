Cardio Excercise

    What Is Cardio Exercise?

    Your heart beats faster. You breathe faster and deeper. And you sweat. Well, it could be because you’ve been working the big muscles in your legs, arms, and hips for a long time. When these major muscles are engaged in exercise, the rate of respiration is increased to generate energy. In turn, the need for more oxygen leads to increased respiration and heart rate. And one such form of activity is called cardio exercise or cardio for short.

    Why should you do cardiovascular exercise?

    Cardio exercise uses the large muscles of your body in movement over a sustained period of time, keeping your heart rate to at least 50-percent of its maximum level. With regular aerobic exercises, you will have a stronger cardiovascular system, with more capillaries delivering more oxygen to the cells in your muscles. You will also enjoy increased stamina and endurance with each passing session.

Specific benefits of cardio exercise include:

    Improved heart health:

    When you engage in 30-60 minutes of cardiovascular exercise a day, you can build stronger muscles, including heart muscle, help control blood pressure, improve HDL (good cholesterol), reduce anxiety and stress, reducing protein and fat in the blood. contributes to the formation of blood clots, prevents heart disease, lowers blood sugar and controls diabetes.

    Enhanced brain health:

    With regular cardiovascular exercise, the areas of the brain that control memory and thinking skills increase in volume or size. Regular cardiovascular activity also reduces the rate of brain size decline in older adults, thereby improving their cognitive function. But cardio can also help you get a good night’s sleep, which is essential for your mental health.

    Improved mood and energy:

    Cardiovascular exercise triggers an increase in the secretion of endorphins, the neurochemicals that cause feelings of euphoria. Cardio also increases the production of mood-enhancing hormones such as dopamine, serotonin, and norepinephrine. With an improved mood, you feel more energetic and ready to complete your daily activities. But the increased hormone release also reduces stress, increases stamina, increases energy, and improves memory and mental focus.

    Weight regulation:

    By increasing your heart rate into your target heart rate zone, which is the area where the body burns the most calories, cardio helps burn excess calories and control weight. Exercises like walking, swimming, and jogging will burn excess calories over time, while moderate to high-intensity cardio burns a lot of calories per session. Examples of cardiovascular exercises that are very effective for weight loss include jumping rope, stair running, walking, rowing, cycling, and high-intensity interval training (HIIT).

    Regular exercise increases the release of antibodies and white blood cells, which improve the body’s ability to fight infections. In fact, cardio protects against a number of diseases, including hypertension, stroke, osteoporosis, diabetes, and heart disease.
